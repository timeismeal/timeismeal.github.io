Top vs Side Navigation: A Duel for the Culinary Ages

Chad

07 Oct 2023 • 1 min read

When designing Time is Meal, I had to make a choice: go with the sleek, straight-to-the-point top navigation, or the robust, can-carry-everything side navigation. Ding, ding, ding! Let the match begin!

The Tale of the Tape

Top Navigation

  • Pros: Doesn't hog space, stays at the top where it's easy to spot.
  • Ideal for: Most websites, from tiny to huge. Especially those like mine that aim for simplicity and clarity.

Side Navigation

  • Pros: Perfect for when you have a lot to show. It's like the utility belt of web design.
  • Ideal for: More complex sites, where users might need a bit more guidance or have many options to choose from.

Given that Time is Meal's mission is straightforward – helping users whip up recipes and generate shopping lists – I leaned towards the top navigation. It's like choosing a quick stir-fry over a 5-course meal; both have their place, but one just felt right for now.

I know side navigation might be sulking in the corner, feeling a bit left out. But who knows? As Time is Meal grows and evolves, there might be a spot for it in the future. For now, though, top navigation is leading the dance.

To sum it up, it's all about picking the right tool for the job. And for Time is Meal's debut, top navigation was the chef's choice.
